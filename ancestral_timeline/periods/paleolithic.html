Ust'-Ishim Individual
~45,000 BCE, Siberia
One of the oldest modern human genomes, representing early Out-of-Africa populations before major divergence events.
Basal Connection: Represents deep ancestral lineages shared by all non-African populations
Technical Details:

Sample shows ~2% Neanderthal ancestry, consistent with early admixture. Provides calibration point for mutation rates and demographic models. Low coverage but high quality ancient DNA.

European Hunter-Gatherers
~40,000 - 8,000 BCE
Early European populations including Western (WHG) and Eastern (EHG) hunter-gatherers who formed the substrate for later European populations.
Medium Connection: Contributes to European ancestry components in K=27 model
Key Sites:

Kostenki (Russia), Mal'ta (Siberia), Loschbour (Luxembourg). WHG and EHG show distinct ancestry patterns that persist into modern Europeans through Neolithic farmer admixture.

Caucasus Hunter-Gatherers
~13,000 - 8,000 BCE
Populations from the Caucasus region that contributed significantly to later Steppe pastoralists and through them to modern Europeans and South Asians.
High Connection: Major component in Caucasus-related ancestry
Genetic Impact:

CHG ancestry forms ~50% of Yamnaya Steppe pastoralists, creating the "Steppe" component visible in modern Europeans. Key to understanding Indo-European expansion patterns.
