Yamnaya Steppe Pastoralists
~3,300 - 2,600 BCE
Nomadic pastoralists from the Pontic-Caspian Steppe who spread across Europe and Asia, likely bringing Indo-European languages and revolutionizing European genetics.
High Connection: Steppe ancestry component visible in modern Europeans
Genetic Composition:

Yamnaya = ~50% EHG (Eastern Hunter-Gatherer) + ~50% CHG (Caucasus Hunter-Gatherer). This mixture created the "Steppe" component that spread into Europe and Central/South Asia.

Bell Beaker Culture
~2,800 - 1,800 BCE
A widespread cultural phenomenon across Europe associated with distinctive pottery and the spread of Steppe ancestry into Western Europe.
Medium Connection: Represents Steppe ancestry integration in Western Europe
Population Turnover:

Bell Beaker expansion represents ~90% population replacement in Britain and significant turnover across Western Europe, marking the end of Neolithic populations.

Minoan Civilization
~2,700 - 1,100 BCE
Bronze Age civilization on Crete that remained largely isolated from Steppe migrations, preserving earlier Neolithic ancestry patterns.
Low Connection: Represents pre-Steppe European populations
Genetic Isolation:

Minoans show ~13% Steppe ancestry compared to ~50% in contemporary mainland Europeans, demonstrating island isolation effects and population continuity.
