1. Overview

Hidden Lineage is committed to protecting your privacy, especially when it comes to genetic information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you visit our website or use our services.

üõ°Ô∏è Privacy First Approach

We believe genetic information is among the most sensitive personal data. Our privacy practices are designed with this principle at the core, prioritizing transparency, user control, and data minimization.

2. Current Phase: Research Preview

Hidden Lineage is currently in research preview mode. This means:

  • No DNA upload capability: We do not currently accept user-submitted genetic data
  • Single sample analysis: The website displays analysis of one anonymized genome for demonstration purposes
  • No personal genetic data collected: We cannot analyze your DNA at this time
  • Website analytics only: We only collect standard web analytics data

‚úÖ Current Data Protection

Since we don't currently handle personal genetic data, your genetic privacy is fully protected during this research preview phase.

3. What Data We Collect

3.1 Website Analytics Data

We collect standard website analytics information through Google Analytics:

  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • General geographic location (country/region level)
  • Device type, browser type, and screen resolution
  • How you arrived at our website (referral sources)
  • Anonymous usage patterns and navigation behavior

3.2 Contact Information

If you contact us directly via email, we collect:

  • Your email address
  • Name (if provided)
  • Message content
  • Any additional information you choose to share

3.3 No Genetic Data (Current Phase)

We currently do not collect, store, or process any personal genetic information.

4. How We Use Your Data

4.1 Website Analytics

  • Understand how visitors use our website
  • Improve website performance and user experience
  • Analyze which content is most valuable to users
  • Debug technical issues and optimize loading times

4.2 Research and Development

  • Understand user interest in genetic archaeology
  • Gauge demand for different features and capabilities
  • Plan future development priorities

4.3 Communication

  • Respond to your inquiries and support requests
  • Provide updates about the project (if you've requested them)
  • Collaborate with researchers and potential contributors

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

5.1 Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services:

  • Google Analytics: Website usage analytics (anonymized data)
  • GitHub: Code repository hosting (public methodology)
  • Email services: For communication (if you contact us)

5.2 No Sale or Commercial Sharing

üö´ We Never Sell Data

We do not sell, rent, or commercially share any user data with third parties. We are not a commercial entity and have no financial incentive to monetize user information.

5.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, such as:

  • In response to valid legal process (subpoenas, court orders)
  • To protect the rights, safety, or property of users or others
  • In connection with legal investigations

6. Data Security Measures

6.1 Current Security

  • HTTPS encryption: All website traffic is encrypted in transit
  • Minimal data collection: We only collect what's necessary
  • No sensitive data storage: No genetic or personal health information stored
  • Regular updates: Website infrastructure kept up to date

6.2 Future Genetic Data Security (Planned)

When we implement DNA upload capabilities, security measures will include:

  • End-to-end encryption: Genetic data encrypted during upload and storage
  • Local processing options: Ability to analyze data without uploading to servers
  • Automatic deletion: Genetic data deleted after analysis completion
  • Access controls: Strict limits on who can access genetic information
  • Audit logging: Complete records of data access and processing

7. Your Rights and Choices

7.1 Current Rights

  • Analytics opt-out: You can disable Google Analytics tracking
  • Contact data deletion: Request removal of any contact information
  • Data access: Request information about what data we have
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate information

7.2 Future Genetic Data Rights (Planned)

When genetic analysis becomes available, you will have:

  • Informed consent: Clear explanation before any genetic analysis
  • Immediate deletion: Right to delete genetic data at any time
  • Download results: Export your analysis results
  • Opt-out of research: Choose whether results contribute to research
  • Local processing: Option to analyze data without uploading

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Genetic Privacy

Genetic information can potentially identify you and your relatives. When upload capabilities become available, we will provide detailed information about risks and protections before you submit any genetic data.

8. Future Upload Platform

8.1 Planned Privacy Features

  • Privacy by design: Data protection built into system architecture
  • Consent management: Granular control over data use
  • Pseudonymization: Genetic data separated from identifying information
  • Retention limits: Automatic deletion of genetic data after processing
  • Export capabilities: Download your results before deletion

8.2 Research Participation

Future options for contributing to genetic archaeology research:

  • Voluntary participation: Opt-in only for research contributions
  • Aggregate statistics only: No individual-level data shared
  • Withdrawal rights: Remove your contribution at any time
  • Transparency: Clear information about how data contributes to research
