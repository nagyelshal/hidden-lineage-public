About Hidden Lineage

The story behind genetic archaeology and the quest to understand deep ancestry

The Story Behind the Project

Hidden Lineage began with a simple question: what can modern DNA reveal about ancient human history that commercial tests miss?

🧬 The Inspiration

After receiving commercial DNA results that showed broad continental categories, I wondered what deeper stories my genome might tell. The gap between what population genetics research revealed and what was accessible to individuals sparked this project.

🔬 The Technical Challenge

Building a research-grade analysis pipeline required integrating the Allen Ancient DNA Resource with modern population genetics software. The goal: make academic-level genetic archaeology accessible to everyone.

📊 The Breakthrough

When I1877, a 6,500-year-old Neolithic farmer from Turkey, appeared as my closest ancient genetic match in PCA analysis, it transformed an abstract concept into a personal connection across 8,500 years of human history.

Technical Background

Educational Context: This project emerged from software architecture experience combined with fascination for population genetics research. While I'm not a trained geneticist, the methodology follows established academic protocols from leading ancient DNA laboratories.

Academic Inspiration: The work of David Reich Lab (Harvard), Pontus Skoglund (Crick Institute), and other pioneers in ancient DNA analysis provided the computational framework and reference datasets that make this analysis possible.

Technical Philosophy

Hidden Lineage prioritizes transparency, reproducibility, and honest communication about limitations.

🔓 Open Science

All methods, parameters, and data sources are documented. The goal is full reproducibility - anyone should be able to verify and extend this work.

⚖️ Honest Limitations

Chr22-only analysis is clearly disclosed. Reference panel imbalances are acknowledged. Results are presented as research insights, not definitive ancestry claims.

🎯 Complementary Approach

Hidden Lineage doesn't compete with commercial tests - it complements them. Different questions require different tools and timeframes.

Quality Control Measures

Data Filtering: Ancient samples filtered for <5% contamination, UDG-treated libraries preferred, pseudo-haploid calling to avoid reference bias.

Statistical Validation: 5-fold cross-validation for K selection, multiple random seeds per run, convergence criteria (log-likelihood delta < 0.0001).

Interpretation Caution: Components treated as statistical clusters rather than literal populations. High-K artifacts acknowledged and discussed.
